**M Arch thesis · University of Colorado Denver · Fall 2025 · 3,675 sq ft · Thomas Jefferson's Poplar Forest**

This project proposes a new visitors center that balances presence and restraint,
creating a dignified civic threshold without overshadowing the historical
landmark. The design draws from classical and Jeffersonian architectural
principles.

The spatial organization centers on an octagonal reception hall with overhead
illumination. The site strategy separates vehicle and bus drop-off zones while
incorporating hedge gardens, a reflecting pool, and seating areas. A grand Ionic
portico marks the main entrance, leading to a foyer that distributes visitors to
a lecture room, showroom, or the central octagon. A gift shop opens to outdoor
gathering areas, with flanked corridors connecting to secondary porticos and
staff-only second-floor access.

The octagonal room uses frosted glass floor panels to let light from a skylight
filter downward, maintaining privacy while solving interior lighting challenges.
Conceptually constructed in wood and stone, the building incorporates classical
elements including Ionic columns, broken pediments, and hierarchical window
proportioning. Documented in AutoCAD, modeled in Revit, and rendered in
Twinmotion to Design Development level.
