**BS Architecture · University of Colorado Denver · Spring 2023**

Completed as part of a BS architecture degree, this collection examines wall
assemblies, cladding systems, and critical building-envelope conditions. The
work features hand-drawn technical studies drafted to scale on graph paper,
emphasizing line-weight hierarchy, material hatching, and precise annotation.

The project investigates the complexity of wall construction through multiple
drawing studies. Systems covered include stucco with control joints, EIFS
detailing, brick veneer, GFRC panels, precast curtain wall connections, and
mullion anchors. Drawings were executed at scales of 1/8", ¼", and ½" to show
both overall systems and detailed junction conditions.

The work demonstrates an understanding of material transitions, moisture-control
strategies, and attachment methods. All ten pages — covering envelope
conditions, door details, parapet flashing, and stair construction — were
completed entirely by hand, showcasing technical fluency in how buildings are
assembled.
