**Residential architecture thesis · University of Colorado Denver · Spring 2025 · 1,538 sq ft**

This residence explores how modest scale can still achieve architectural
grandeur through classical inspiration. The design centers on a rotunda crowned
by a cupola, organizing the living spaces symmetrically around this focal point.

The layout extends three primary axes from the rotunda: dining and kitchen to
the left, the living room to the right, and bedrooms to the rear. The living
room accommodates formal seating and a grand piano. A perpendicular hallway
separates a guest bedroom from the main suite, each with its own bathroom and
deck access.

The design employs "stacked geometry" — an octagonal cupola above a square
widow's walk — to reinforce hierarchy. Materials include brick walls, stone
quoining, shutters, and classical trim. The project emphasizes symmetry, layered
geometry, and light to elevate residential architecture at a modest scale.
