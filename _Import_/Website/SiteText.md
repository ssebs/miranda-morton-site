**The Hangar at Central Park** *JLA Architects*  
**Short description:** A 4-story, 259-unit market-rate multifamily development by JLA Architects in Denver's Central Park neighborhood, currently under construction for Clear Sky Capital.  
**Summary:** The Hangar replaces a former airport hangar on one of the last remaining parcels in North America's largest master-planned community. The four-story wrap building draws on a "Nordic Chic" aesthetic, with a muted material palette anchored by fiber cement, brick, glulam timber, and cable railings. I independently produced the project master plan and contributed across most phases from schematic design through construction documentation — developing floor plans, reflected ceiling plans, exterior elevations, interior elevations, wall sections, and enlarged unit plans, with particular attention to accessibility compliance and building envelope coordination.  
**Role & contribution:**

* Independently produced the rendered project master plan  
* Developed construction documents across SD, DD, and CD phases  
* Produced enlarged unit plans and interior elevations for multiple unit types  
* Coordinated ADA Type A/B accessibility requirements per ANSI A117.1  
* Contributed to wall section and building envelope documentation

**Ravenfield** *JLA Architects*  
**Short description:** A 3-story, 46-unit LIHTC affordable senior housing development by JLA Architects in Brighton, CO for the Brighton Housing Authority, currently in design.  
**Summary:** Ravenfield is designed to integrate into its surrounding residential neighborhood while fostering connection and community among its senior residents. Amenities include a community room, library, business center, coffee bar, rooftop terrace, and direct access to walking paths. I contributed during schematic design, participating in client meetings and facilitating a community design charrette with neighboring residents. Using scaled site elements, I gathered feedback that directly informed the project's site organization and building placement.  
**Role & contribution:**

* Contributed during schematic design phase  
* Participated in client meetings  
* Facilitated a community design charrette using scaled site elements to gather resident input  
* Community feedback directly shaped the project's site organization and design direction

**Poplar Forest** *Grad Studio 5 — M.Arch, University of Colorado Denver*  
**Short description:** A 3,608 SF visitor center for Thomas Jefferson's Poplar Forest estate in Lynchburg, VA, designed for Graduate Studio 5 and organized around a central octagonal reception hall informed by classical architectural precedent.  
**Summary:** The design takes its cues from the classical language of the historic main house while remaining deliberately subordinate to it. Sited along the estate's primary axis, the building reads as a formal but restrained civic structure — an Ionic-columned portico framing the entrance, with flanking porches and symmetrical massing that defer to Poplar Forest itself. The first-floor centers on an octagonal reception hall connecting the public program; the second floor contains offices arranged around a central light well. Daylight enters the hall through a skylight and frosted glass floor, creating a luminous interior that contrasts with the measured exterior. Proportional analysis informed the placement and sizing of every major element, from column spacing to window positions. Produced in AutoCAD, Revit, Twinmotion, and Photoshop.  
**Role & contribution:**

* Solo project created for Graduate Studio 5; responsible for all design, documentation, and visualization  
* Developed site plan, floor plans, roof plan, all four elevations, building sections, wall section, interior elevations, and trim details  
* Produced proportional analysis diagrams applying the golden ratio to both plan and elevation  
* Produced exterior and interior renderings in Twinmotion and Photoshop

**Modest Monumentality** *Design Studio 4 — M.Arch, University of Colorado Denver*  
**Short description:** A 1,543 SF single-family residence in Morrison, CO designed for Graduate Studio 4, applying classical principles of symmetry, procession, and proportion at a domestic scale.  
**Summary:** The design centers on a rotunda capped by an octagonal cupola and square widow's walk, which brings natural light down into the heart of the plan. Wings extend from the rotunda to accommodate dining and kitchen on one side and a great room on the other, while a rear wing contains the primary suite and a guest bedroom. A formal parterre garden extends the building's axis into the landscape. Proportional analysis — including golden ratio studies applied to the elevation and plan — governed the sizing and placement of structural bays, windows, and classical details. Column capital studies and trim details were developed at large scale, demonstrating close engagement with the classical orders. Produced in AutoCAD, Revit, Twinmotion, and Photoshop.  
**Role & contribution:**

* Solo project created for Graduate Studio 4; responsible for all design, documentation, and visualization  
* Developed site plan, floor plans, all four elevations, building sections, and proportional analysis diagrams  
* Produced large-scale column capital studies, trim profiles, and classical detail drawings  
* Produced exterior renderings in Twinmotion and Photoshop

**Detail Studies** *Construction Practices: Building Envelope — B.S. Architecture, University of Colorado Denver*  
**Short description:** A series of hand-drawn, scaled technical studies produced for an undergraduate building envelope course, covering wall assemblies, cladding systems, and connection details across a range of construction types.  
**Summary:** Each drawing was drafted by hand to scale with consistent line weights, hatching, and annotations, demonstrating close study of how buildings are actually assembled. The series spans wood light-frame and cold-formed steel construction, multiple cladding systems, roof assemblies, and stair construction — with each detail developed from first principles rather than traced from reference. Systems covered include a WLF wall section from rafter to foundation, stucco cladding with double-V control joint, EIFS with joint detail, brick veneer with shelf angle and flashing, a precast curtain wall panel connection, a GFRC panel with flex anchor and tie-back, mullion anchors to spandrel beam, a door detail with steel lintel and brick veneer, parapet flashing and coping, and stair framing with balusters and face stringer trim.  
**Role & contribution:**

* Solo coursework; all details drawn by hand to scale  
* Developed 10 details spanning WLF, CFS, masonry veneer, EIFS, precast and GFRC cladding, curtain wall, roofing, and stair assemblies  
* Each drawing annotated with full component labeling and produced at appropriate drawing scale (1/8" to 1/2" \= 1')
